# Aviation Accident Analysis

###    BUSINESS UNDESRTANDING

This project is carried out on behalf of an airline insurance company that wants to expand into aircraft insurance. Before they start writing policies, they need to know which aircraft are safest to insure  and which conditions make accidents more deadly. The dataset covers over 17,000 aviation accidents from 1983 to 2023, including information on injuries, aircraft damage, weather, engine type and more.


### 1. Problem Statement

The insurance company has no data-driven basis for deciding which aircraft makes and models to insure or how to price risk. This project analyses historical accident records to identify which manufacturers and specific models have the lowest rates of passenger injury and aircraft destruction giving the insurer a solid foundation for their decisions.

### 2. Metric of Success

By identifying the safest aircraft makes and models and the conditions that lead to worse outcomes I will consider this project a success. Success is measured by producing clear, evidence-backed recommendations for both small aircraft (≤20 passengers) and large aircraft (>20 passengers).

### 3. Specific Objective

To identify which aircraft makes and models from 1983 to 2023 have the lowest rates of serious/fatal passenger injuries and aircraft destruction in the event of an accident.

### 4. Other Objectives

* To determine whether weather conditions (clear vs poor visibility) affect how severe accidents are
* To determine whether engine type is associated with higher injury rates
* To split the analysis into small and large aircraft so the insurer gets relevant recommendations for each category

### 5. Research Questions

a. Which aircraft manufacturers (makes) have the lowest average injury rate when accidents happen?

b. Which specific aircraft models are the safest, looking only at models with enough accident records to draw reliable conclusions?

c. Do accidents in poor visibility (IMC conditions) result in more injuries and aircraft destruction than accidents in clear weather (VMC)?

d. Does engine type have a meaningful effect on accident severity?




### FINDINGS
**Large aircraft:** Boeing and McDonnell Douglas models (especially the 777 and 757) consistently show the lowest injury rates

**Small aircraft:** Cessna 172SP and Diamond DA-20 stand out as the safest small plane models

**Weather matters:** Accidents in poor visibility (IMC) are significantly more severe than those in clear conditions

**Engine type matters:** Jet-powered aircraft have far lower injury rates than piston-engine planes largely because jets are operated commercially with professional crews and stricter maintenance
